About Our Data

This application uses multiple datasets to calculate the number of trees needed to absorb a country's carbon emissions on a specific day. The calculation takes into account different tree species' carbon absorption rates, their prevalence in different countries, and daily carbon emission data.

How We Use The Data

1

Tree Species Data

We use the GBIF Backbone Taxonomy to identify tree species and their scientific classifications. This helps us match common names with scientific names and ensure consistency across datasets.

2

Biomass Calculation

The Forest Biomass dataset provides information on tree growth rates and biomass accumulation. We use this to calculate how much biomass different tree species can accumulate over time.

3

Carbon Density

The Wood Carbon Database gives us information on how much carbon different tree species can store in their tissues. We use this to convert biomass to carbon sequestration potential.

4

Country Emissions

The Carbon Monitor dataset provides daily carbon emission data by country. We use this to calculate how many trees would be needed to offset those emissions.

5

Final Calculation

By combining all this data, we can calculate how many trees of a particular species would be needed to absorb a country's carbon emissions for a specific day.
